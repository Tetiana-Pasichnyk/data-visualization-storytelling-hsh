## 📚 Overview

This folder contains detailed planning materials, instructor guides, and comprehensive documentation for the "Data Visualization & Storytelling" course. These materials are intended for instructors who need in-depth information about course structure, implementation, and assessment.

---

## 🎓 Detailed Course Overview

### Course Goal
Enable students to present results convincingly and communicate data-driven recommendations.

### Structure
- **9 Teaching Blocks** (1 day each)
- **2-3 Units per Block** (90 minutes each)
- **Weekly Assignments** (60% of grade)
- **Final Project** (40% of grade)

### Main Tools
- **Primary:** Matplotlib, Seaborn, Plotly, Pandas
- **Secondary:** Bokeh, Streamlit, Power BI, Tableau

### Time Formats
1. **Full Semester:** 11 weeks (recommended)
2. **Intensive Course:** 4 weeks
3. **Weekend Workshop:** 4 weekends
4. **Self-Study:** Flexible

## 📊 Assessment Framework

### Grade Distribution
- **Weekly Assignments:** 60% (9 × 6-7%)

### Grading Criteria
- **Technical Implementation:** 40%
- **Visual Design:** 30%
- **Documentation:** 20%
- **Creativity:** 10%

### Passing Requirements
- **Minimum Score:** 50%
- **Assignments:** At least 5 of 9 submitted
- **Final Project:** Mandatory

## 📅 Time Format Options

### 1. Full Semester (11 Weeks) - RECOMMENDED
- Week 1: Block 1
- Weeks 2-10: Blocks 2-9 (one per week)
- Week 11: Final project presentations
- **Total:** 27 units × 90 min = 40.5 hours

### 2. Intensive Course (4 Weeks)
- Week 1: Blocks 1-2
- Week 2: Blocks 3-5
- Week 3: Blocks 6-8
- Week 4: Block 9 + Final project
- **Total:** Compressed schedule

### 3. Weekend Workshop (4 Weekends)
- Weekend 1: Blocks 1-2
- Weekend 2: Blocks 3-5
- Weekend 3: Blocks 6-8
- Weekend 4: Block 9 + Final project
- **Total:** 8 days

### 4. Self-Study (Flexible)
- Students work at their own pace
- Weekly check-ins recommended
- Asynchronous support
- **Total:** Variable
